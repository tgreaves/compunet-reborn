 I've been reading books again, & this
week it's "The Road to Gandolfo" by
Robert Ludlum, from the public library.
He writes well: usually blood 'n thunder
stuff, but this one is a comedy. Here's
bits from the start of Chap.12

 He let himself into Annie's hotel
suite. "If that's not you Sam, I've got
a problem," she called from the bedroom.
 "It's me. All your problems are happy
ones."
"I like those kind."
 Annie was sitting up reading a paper-
back. "It's a marvellous history of
Henry the Eighth's wives. That man was a
monster!"
 "Not really. A lot of his problems were
geopolitical."

 "In his crotch they were!"
 "That's more historically sound than
you may think. How about a drink?"

Annie says, later....
 "The operator said there was a call for
you from General Hawkins in Washington.
I think the rank & city made him
nervous, so I told him not to worry: I'd
talk to the old fart. He liked that."
