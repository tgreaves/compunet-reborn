        The ICC meet (day one)          
                            by  Fogg    
                                        
 Day one of the ICC meet see's Top-Cat  
 and Bil settled into Fogg's computer   
 room, as widely predicted the two are  
 locked into a continual bicker! Are    
 these two actually friends? Despite    
 a few useless protests from Fogg, the  
 back is soon off his cherished modem   
 and TC, has within seconds wrenched a  
 seemingly ultra delicate multi         
 terminaled chip out of it.             
                                        
 Working like beavers they whack all    
 manner of suspicious looking cartrid-  
 ges into every available orifice of    
 Fogg's cherished equipment.The disc-   
 drive howls with agony and belches     
 forth noises,the like of which Fogg    
 has only heard when wrestling with     
 knackered combine harvesters!

   PORNOGRAPHY? FOUL LANGUAGE? ABUSE?   
                                        
      A Personal Opinion by Gnome       
                                        
  Our esteemed Editor, under his ID of  
 FEEDBACK, has invited comment on this  
 thorny subject: should those who write 
 such material on the net be warned,    
 punished, banned or censored? I have   
 already made a short reply in Arena &  
 would like to amplify this a bit.      
                                        
  Pornography is OUT. Having said that, 
 how do we define it? The highest court 
 in the land has difficulty doing this, 
 but the judgment usually hinges on     
 what would offend Mr Average?  I think 
 those who choose to upload material of 
 "doubtful" nature should expect to be  
 dealt with according to the decision   

--------------------------------------
  ICC NEWS                              
  Issue 18     30th April      Page 05  

 of Cnet management in an arbitrary     
 fashion. If they sail too close to the 
 wind they must expect a sudden squall  
 to capsize them. I see no need for a   
 warning: the editor should do what he  
 wishes at the time. This will depend   
 on the context, and perhaps even which 
 dir it was put into. And I believe     
 there should be no argument. I do not  
 believe any uploader can be in much    
 doubt what sort of material would be   
 censored. Breasts are ok in some dirs. 
 Explicit sex is out.                   
                                        
  Abuse is slightly trickier. Friends   
 are at liberty to say what they like   
 to each other in the privacy of a mbx  
 but even "stupid fool" is ground for a 
 complaint by a stranger. It's not on   
 to make personal remarks about someone 
 you don't know well in public.But it's 
 pretty silly to complain to the Editor 


--------------------------------------
  ICC NEWS                              
  Issue 18     30th April      Page 06  

 about the first incident. Sensible     
 action is to complain first to the     
 writer, then to the club they may      
 belong to; and only as a final resort  
 to Cnet management if the abuse is     
 continued.                             
                                        
   Now for this "bad language" affair.  
 So far I haven't seen any 'bad' words  
 on Cnet which I haven't previously     
 heard from kids of 6 and younger. It's 
 not clever to use these WORDS to which 
 I refer: and it can be very boring. It 
 can however be decorative or amusing   
 in some contexts. I feel that there's  
 really nothing to these WORDS. What    
 offends people is the time & place     
 where they are used. I was taught not  
 to swear in front of ladies, nor in    
 public, because it offends people. And 
 I do not want to offend anyone. "There 
 is a time & place for everything" and  


--------------------------------------
  ICC NEWS                              
  Issue 18     30th April      Page 07  

 the public pages on Compunet are not   
 normally the place for 4-letter anglo  
 saxon words. But I do think that one   
 can make exceptions: and I support one 
 well-known reviewer in his use of a    
 few of the less shattering WORDS such  
 as piss, fart, smelly-pants simply     
 because he not only uses them in a     
 humorous manner, but has managed to    
 incorporate them into a "style" which, 
 though I know it offends some, has     
 become his own art-form! Frankly he    
 makes me giggle openly - not snigger   
 behind the bike-shed.                  
  We should all face the fact that Cnet 
 is a robust, lively, hearty society.   
 It's a forum where opinion is aired as 
 well as information provided.          
  So I would say to Feedback, "Do what  
 you think right at the time. Do it     
 fearlessly and quickly. ICC will       
 support you, and I hope Nick will too.

     The ICC Meet according to BIL      
     ==============================                                        

 Dave (TC) and myself had the dubious   
 honour of having to travel further     
 than anyone else at the meet. So I     
 spent a pleasant eight hours trying to 
 compete with the conversation power of 
 Paula Abdul and INXS. When we arrived  
 at Foggy's, we naturally tore his      
 equipment to bits - with him merely a  
 horrified spectator.                   
                                        
 Day two, and people started to arrive. 
 First off the mark was GNOME (are you  
 surprised?), then Dral and Mrs Dral.   
 Next came TJ and Fi (that's Fee!) who  
 was out in force, flogging copies of   
 her Idiots Guide to Fed. Being a fully 
 qualified Idiot in the art of many     
 things, but not Fed, I resigned myself 
 to the inevitable and bought a copy.   
 When everyone arrived - the real fun   


--------------------------------------
  ICC NEWS                              
  Issue 18     30th April      Page 09  

 started! The people at the meet were:  
 Roy, Fogg, Dral, Gnome, TJ, Phil,      
 Diesel, Tc and myself - as well as Mrs 
 Dral who seemed remarkably sturdy in   
 patience when you consider what was to 
 come.                                  
                                        
 Seven hours of talking, drinking,      
 eating, reading, shouting, jumping up  
 and down, and generally having a wild  
 and whacky time using our neatly laid  
 out agenda (a la TJ) as a feeble       
 excuse.                                
                                        
 There were lots of things covered,     
 ranging from how to make C'net look    
 prettier, to how to keep Roy on the    
 'net for a few more months at least.   
 It was now time for Roy+Phil to        
 disappear (work work work), leaving    
 the rest of us to scoff all the        
 delicious delicacies - and log-on!     


--------------------------------------
  ICC NEWS                              
  Issue 18     30th April      Page 10  

 It was more or less at this point,     
 that Sunday news was Pistponed, due to 
 many of us being too inebriated to     
 type sensibly! (that orange juice is   
 strong stuff!)                         
                                        
 We then had a very enjoyable hour on   
 Pline - TJ risked his reputation to    
 the limit! Then we all went to bed.    
 (not together stupid!)                 
                                        
 Next morning, and it was time to fight 
 for your life in the kitchen, grabbing 
 every spare morsel that was going! And 
 going is the word, because after an    
 ICC photo and film shot, it was time   
 to disappear with Fi, TJ and TC to     
 catch the train in Birmingham (which   
 we naturally missed!). Home again, and 
 Bil ponders on the usefullness of the  
 venture, and then decides he enjoyed   
 it!                              BIL

              Online???                 
                                        
 Once again, the online QUIZ was        
 postponed last night. Once again it    
 was due to problems outwith our        
 control. Once again contestants are    
 wondering if it will ever happen. Once 
 again we apologise humbly, but once    
 again we're sure you understand that   
 there was nothing we could do about    
 it.                                    
                                        
 Sadly, Roy will not be organising the  
 QUIZ when it does take place, as he is 
 unfortunately leaving the 'net to      
 spend more time with his family. The   
 ICC and Cnet will miss you greatly     
 Roy. Come back soon. Meanwhile, there  
 is no set date for the QUIZ to resume.
